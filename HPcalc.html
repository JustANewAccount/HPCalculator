Monthly Interest Income — Rule 78 vs US GAAP (EIR)

Annual P&L Impact — Difference (Over/Under)

Rule 78 (Sum-of-Digits): Interest allocated using digit weights: Month n gets weight = (N−n+1) ÷ Σ(1…N) × Total Interest. Front-loads interest. NOT permitted for published financial statements under US GAAP, IFRS 9, or FRS 109.
US GAAP / IFRS 9 — Effective Interest Rate (EIR): Interest = Opening Principal × Monthly EIR. The EIR is solved iteratively (IRR) such that PV of all instalments equals loan amount. Constant yield over the life of the loan.
Difference Analysis: Positive = Rule 78 overstates income vs EIR (earlier periods). Negative = Rule 78 understates income vs EIR (later periods). Total difference = zero over full term.
Annual P&L Restatement: Summarised by financial year end. Positive Over/(Under) means Rule 78 overstated income — must reduce retained earnings on restatement. Negative means Rule 78 understated — must increase HP Debtors.
